It is the word that can empower women to raise their voice to take everything they deserve as a human being. It is the key to release women from the oppressive expectations of the society, family, and significant others. It gives women the strength to pursue the life they want and raise their voices about the issues they face. Guys it is already 2018, women should be able to be who they are.

The Japanese government has been trying to make "The Promotion of Gender Equality" in the workforce into reality. More women are working in companies while also taking care of her family as a housewife. Recently more companies have been allowing women to take parental leave. There are also more women who have been able to pursue their career. However, I still feel like feminism does not exist in this country. Why?..because the common understanding among the people in the society we live in has not changed a bit. Women are still expected to live up to all the standards of being a housewife and a career woman at the same time.

This society has pressured employed women to decide either leave their job or give up her family, while guys are able to choose both without any pressure. As women, we often choose "Family" over other opportunities without considering the 20+ years of education and skills we have earned. Our right is neglected and sacrificed for the sake of the family. However, Many women fantasize about their lives becoming complete by their husband and children. Is the belief in being a housewife worth throwing away our individual faith? It is too inconsiderate to shut all the women in the same box as this. Do we all need to find our prince charming before twenty-five and becoming a wife and mother by thirty? Diverse opportunities should be provided to men and women equally.

Housewives technically don't have status in society because they don't earn money. While career women criticize themselves not being married or being undesirable. Even those who have managed

both, have the guilt that they have not done things perfectly and needed to have asked for help and supports. No matter what women choose, guilt chases after them. Must we feel stressed about it just because we are women? Why can't we enjoy our own lifestyle without drawing so much criticism?

If a girl wants to pursue her education in M.A. or P.h.d, why don't we support her career path? There is no need to advise her that it would negatively affect her marriage. If there are women who want to work in politics and have kids, why don't we let them work with their baby or make have more facilities to support them, not just telling her that she is too stubborn if she chooses her career over her family or pressuring her to work like a guy. There may also be women who dream to become the wife of a partner she loves and support her family at home. Why don't we think her as a valued home provider instead of just living off her husband? We should be happy for her making a huge decision like this but not because this is the role that women should have to fulfill. If there is a girl who raises her voice to save victims of sexual abuses or harassment, we should support her message. We should not be criticizing her by detracting from the important things she has to say, which the society ignored for a long time.

It's already been decades since the voices of the First-wave Feminist Movement spread. Can we still stand for the situation. All of us can choose any lifestyles we want as well as have the belief we have earned it. We have the right to choose how we live. We should put this fact in our hearts. If you are not comfortable with talking about the change you want to make yet, there are many feminist activists to learn from, Take a look at their work and maybe you will be inspired to take action. Even if there are tiny steps, they will worth it. You know deep down that society needs to change. We have each other to support and believe, so don't be afraid. We can make the world a better place for future generations by believing the power of feminism!

Have you ever thought how important being yourself is? Don't you ever think your decision is manipulated by the societal will not your own will? I often do. And whenever society let me feel that way, I try to tell myself, "who cares?", and I will feel much better.

Japan is sometimes hard to live with. Tattoos are considered as taboo because it is believed tattoos limit people to get a job or even to get in public accommodations. When it comes to a job, people recommend students not to transfer to a foreign university since this also might affect job recruitment without completing 4-year Japanese university. In the fashion magazines, there are lots of letters written such as " mote clothes" " mote action" (mote means being attractive to boys or girls) which makes me think who are we living for? There are also some people who moan about people's interracial relationships. When we try to go out with the tight clothes which I think is the normal clothes in the US, people sneakily watch head to toe.

Job hunting system is likewise. People wear a black suit called 'recruit suit' with a white shirt or blouse with the same hairstyle. Sometimes there are lectures show how to behave or how to do makeup. I think lots of people feel constraint but the reality is that people cannot get out of this comfort zone because most people are afraid of graduating university without any job in their hand by standing out or acting uncooperatively since such synchrony is valued In Japan. A lot of people still think that completing a university without a proper job is shameful and that is the end of the world. I experienced Japanese job hunting too. However, I found out how to not be controlled by the society but control my life with my own will in the US which makes my life more fulfilling and satisfying.

People in America do not care about

others. No matter what I ask for advice, people always told me to do whatever I want, which is the best advice ever because that gives me chance to think.

What we need to remember here is that "doing whatever we want" does not mean being selfish, but always holding faith to live our lives and to be ourselves. Let's just blow away the societal rule and pressure if you think it spoils your individuality but rather, ask yourself what you really want in your life and just tell yourself, "WHO CARES?".

While I was in the States, my roommate once asked me why I wore two bras when I got dressed. Since my breasts were bigger than other girls, I wore a sports bra over a wired bra in order to prevent from myself being seen as a slut. I answered, "To cover my big boobs! I just don't like them." Then she said to me, "It is not your fault to have the size of the breasts, right? Why should you have to feel insecure about having them? There are stupid guys who will stare at you but it is not their business." She also added, "No one should care whether your breasts are bigger or if you even wear bras. It is not okay for guys to treat a stranger as a sex object according to how she dresses. Their opinions of her do not represent who she is." Her opinion helped me realize that I never fully accepted who I am and I was ashamed of how I looked. I decided to dress the way I am comfortable with, not based on what people tell me or expect of me. I felt that something buried in my chest had disappeared. Since then I wear whatever I want, a T-shirt with a bra and denim jeans to embrace my body. It liberates not only my body, but more importaly my heart.

"Why discrimination exists?" and that made her study more about human rights in the grad school in the United States. She supports LGBTQ people, people of color, refugees, and all kinds of human beings and her ultimate goal is to make Japan a better place by speaking up and raising awareness. She hopes that more and more people especially youth find their bright path and some broader perspective of our voice.
